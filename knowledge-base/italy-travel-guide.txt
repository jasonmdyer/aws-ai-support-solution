
Italy Travel Guide

Best Time to Visit: April-June or September-October (mild weather, fewer crowds)
Currency: Euro (EUR)
Language: Italian (English common in tourist areas)
Visa: Schengen zone - 90 days visa-free for US citizens

Top Destinations:
- Rome: Colosseum, Vatican City, Trevi Fountain, Pantheon, Roman Forum, Trastevere neighborhood
- Florence: Uffizi Gallery, Duomo, Ponte Vecchio, Michelangelo's David, Tuscan day trips
- Venice: Grand Canal, St. Mark's Basilica, Murano glass island, gondola rides
- Amalfi Coast: Positano, Ravello, coastal drives, lemon groves
- Cinque Terre: Five colorful coastal villages, hiking trails between towns

Transportation:
- Trenitalia/Italo high-speed trains: Rome to Florence 1.5 hours, Florence to Venice 2 hours
- Book trains early for best prices (from 19 EUR one-way)
- Rent a car only for countryside (Tuscany, Amalfi) - cities are walkable
- Rome metro covers major tourist sites

Budget Breakdown (per person per day):
- Budget: $60-100 (hostels, pizza al taglio, free church visits)
- Mid-range: $150-300 (3-star hotels, trattorias, museum entries)
- Luxury: $500+ (boutique hotels, fine dining, private guides)

Food Must-Try:
- Pizza (Neapolitan style in Naples, Roman style in Rome)
- Fresh pasta (cacio e pepe, carbonara, bolognese)
- Gelato (look for "artigianale" - artisanal)
- Aperitivo hour (drinks + free snacks, 6-8pm)
- Espresso at the bar (standing is cheaper than sitting)

Tips:
- Book major museums in advance (Vatican, Uffizi sell out)
- Restaurants charge "coperto" (cover charge) - this is normal
- Siesta time 1-4pm means some shops close
- Dress code for churches: cover shoulders and knees
- Validate train tickets before boarding at yellow machines
