
Iceland Travel Guide

Best Time to Visit: June-August (midnight sun, all roads open), September-March (Northern Lights, ice caves)
Currency: Icelandic Krona (ISK)
Language: Icelandic (nearly everyone speaks English)
Visa: Schengen zone - 90 days visa-free for US citizens

Top Destinations:
- Reykjavik: Hallgrimskirkja church, Harpa concert hall, vibrant food scene, street art
- Golden Circle: Thingvellir (tectonic plates), Geysir (erupting geysers), Gullfoss (massive waterfall)
- South Coast: Seljalandsfoss and Skogafoss waterfalls, Reynisfjara black sand beach, Vik
- Jokulsarlon: Glacier lagoon with floating icebergs, Diamond Beach
- Snaefellsnes Peninsula: "Iceland in miniature" - Kirkjufell mountain, lava fields, coastal cliffs
- Westfjords: Remote, dramatic fjords, Dynjandi waterfall (summer only)

Transportation:
- Rent a car (essential) - 2WD for Ring Road in summer, 4x4 required for F-roads and winter
- Ring Road (Route 1): 1,322 km loop around entire island, 7-10 days recommended
- No trains in Iceland
- Reykjavik city buses available but limited outside city
- Gas stations every 50-100 km on Ring Road

Budget Breakdown (per person per day):
- Budget: $100-150 (camping, grocery store meals, free natural hot springs)
- Mid-range: $250-400 (guesthouses, restaurants, guided tours)
- Luxury: $500+ (boutique hotels, helicopter tours, private guides)

Must-Do Activities:
- Blue Lagoon or Sky Lagoon geothermal spa: $60-100
- Glacier hiking: $80-120 (guided, required)
- Whale watching from Husavik: $70-90
- Snorkeling/diving Silfra fissure (between tectonic plates): $150-200
- Northern Lights tour (Oct-March): $60-80
- Ice cave exploration (Nov-March): $150-200
- Snowmobile on glacier: $150-200

Tips:
- Weather changes every 5 minutes - layer up, always have waterproof outer layer
- Download offline maps (cell service spotty in remote areas)
- Hot dogs (pylsur) are the national fast food - try one at Baejarins Beztu
- Tap water is pure glacier water - do not buy bottled
- Book Blue Lagoon weeks in advance (sells out)
- F-roads are highland roads only open June-September (require 4x4, often river crossings)
- No tipping culture
- Extremely expensive country - budget $15-25 per restaurant meal minimum
