
Japan Travel Guide

Best Time to Visit: March-May (cherry blossom season) or October-November (fall foliage)
Currency: Japanese Yen (JPY)
Language: Japanese (English signage in major cities)
Visa: Most Western countries get 90-day visa-free entry

Top Destinations:
- Tokyo: Shibuya crossing, Senso-ji temple, Akihabara electronics, Tsukiji Outer Market, Meiji Shrine
- Kyoto: Fushimi Inari shrine (1000 torii gates), Arashiyama bamboo grove, Kinkaku-ji golden temple
- Osaka: Street food capital (takoyaki, okonomiyaki), Dotonbori district, Osaka Castle
- Hiroshima: Peace Memorial Park, Miyajima Island floating torii gate
- Hakone: Hot springs (onsen), Mount Fuji views, Open Air Museum

Transportation:
- Japan Rail Pass (JR Pass): 7-day pass ~$200, unlimited bullet train (shinkansen) travel
- Suica/Pasmo IC cards for local trains and buses
- Bullet train Tokyo to Kyoto: 2 hours 15 minutes

Budget Breakdown (per person per day):
- Budget: $50-80 (hostels, convenience store meals, local trains)
- Mid-range: $150-250 (business hotels, restaurants, some activities)
- Luxury: $400+ (ryokans, kaiseki meals, private tours)

Food Must-Try:
- Ramen (regional styles vary by city)
- Sushi (conveyor belt for budget, omakase for splurge)
- Tempura, yakitori, matcha desserts
- Convenience store onigiri and bento boxes (surprisingly good)

Tips:
- Get a pocket WiFi or eSIM at the airport
- Bow when greeting, remove shoes indoors
- Tipping is not customary and can be considered rude
- Cash is still widely used outside major cities
- Train stations have coin lockers for luggage
