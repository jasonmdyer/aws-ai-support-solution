
Costa Rica Travel Guide

Best Time to Visit: December-April (dry season), May-November (green season - cheaper, some rain)
Currency: Costa Rican Colon (CRC), US dollars widely accepted
Language: Spanish (English common in tourist areas)
Visa: 90-day visa-free entry for US citizens

Top Destinations:
- Arenal: Volcano views, hot springs, hanging bridges, zip-lining
- Monteverde: Cloud forest, wildlife (quetzals, monkeys), canopy tours
- Manuel Antonio: Beach + jungle national park, white-faced monkeys, sloths
- Tamarindo: Surfing, beach town vibe, sunset catamaran tours
- Tortuguero: Sea turtle nesting (July-October), jungle canals by boat
- Osa Peninsula: Remote rainforest, Corcovado National Park (most biodiverse place on earth)

Transportation:
- Rent a 4x4 SUV (many roads are unpaved, river crossings)
- Domestic flights: San Jose to Arenal/Tamarindo ~30 min vs 4-hour drive
- Shared shuttles between major towns: $50-80 per person
- No Uber outside San Jose - use official red taxis

Budget Breakdown (per person per day):
- Budget: $40-70 (hostels, sodas/local restaurants, public buses)
- Mid-range: $100-200 (eco-lodges, guided tours, rental car)
- Luxury: $300+ (boutique resorts, private guides, adventure packages)

Activities and Costs:
- Zip-lining: $50-80
- White water rafting: $80-100
- Guided wildlife tours: $40-60
- Snorkeling/diving: $60-120
- Surfing lessons: $50-70
- Hot springs (Arenal): $20-80 depending on resort

Wildlife You Will See:
- Sloths (two-toed and three-toed)
- Howler monkeys, white-faced capuchins, spider monkeys
- Toucans, scarlet macaws, quetzals
- Poison dart frogs, iguanas, crocodiles
- Sea turtles (seasonal)

Tips:
- Pack layers - microclimates change drastically between coast and mountains
- Bring waterproof bags and quick-dry clothing
- Book Corcovado permits in advance (limited daily visitors)
- "Pura Vida" is the national phrase - means pure life, used as hello/goodbye/thanks
- Tap water is safe to drink in most areas
