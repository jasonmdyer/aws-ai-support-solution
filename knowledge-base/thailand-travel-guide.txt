
Thailand Travel Guide

Best Time to Visit: November-February (cool and dry), avoid April (extreme heat) and September-October (monsoon)
Currency: Thai Baht (THB)
Language: Thai (English common in tourist areas)
Visa: 60-day visa-free entry for US citizens

Top Destinations:
- Bangkok: Grand Palace, Wat Pho, street food on Yaowarat Road, rooftop bars, Chatuchak Weekend Market
- Chiang Mai: Temples (300+), night bazaar, elephant sanctuaries, cooking classes, Doi Suthep
- Phuket: Beaches, island hopping (Phi Phi, James Bond Island), nightlife in Patong
- Krabi/Railay: Rock climbing, Railay Beach (boat access only), Four Islands tour
- Koh Samui/Koh Phangan: Beach relaxation, Full Moon Party, Ang Thong Marine Park
- Pai: Mountain town, hot springs, canyons, backpacker vibe

Transportation:
- Bangkok BTS Skytrain and MRT metro cover the city well
- Domestic flights: Bangkok to Chiang Mai ~1 hour, $30-60 on AirAsia/Nok Air
- Overnight trains: Bangkok to Chiang Mai ~12 hours (sleeper berths available)
- Songthaews (shared red trucks) and tuk-tuks for short distances
- Ferries between southern islands: $10-30

Budget Breakdown (per person per day):
- Budget: $25-50 (hostels, street food, local transport)
- Mid-range: $80-150 (boutique hotels, restaurants, activities)
- Luxury: $250+ (5-star resorts, private longtail boats, spa packages)

Food Must-Try:
- Pad Thai (best from street vendors)
- Green/red/massaman curry
- Tom Yum Goong (spicy shrimp soup)
- Mango sticky rice (dessert)
- Som Tum (papaya salad - ask for farang level spice if you are sensitive to heat)
- Night market grilled meats and seafood

Tips:
- Never disrespect the Thai King or royal family (strict laws)
- Remove shoes before entering temples and homes
- Dress modestly at temples (cover shoulders and knees)
- Negotiate tuk-tuk prices before getting in
- Avoid elephant riding - visit ethical sanctuaries instead
- Street food is safe and delicious - look for busy stalls with high turnover
- Get a Thai massage ($5-10 for one hour)
